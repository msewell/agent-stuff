# OKR Anti-Pattern Checklist

Run this each cycle, ideally during the closeout retro. Answer **Y / N / Partial** for the current cycle. Any Y or Partial gets discussed in the retro and assigned an owner for the next cycle.

---

## Critical — fix before anything else

| # | Anti-pattern | Self-audit question | Standard fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | **Tasks as KRs** | Can any KR be checked off without a metric moving? | Rewrite as outcome with baseline + target |
| 3 | **Comp coupling** | Did any compensation, bonus, or promotion decision directly reference an OKR score? | Decouple. Document the firewall explicitly. |
| 8 | **Set-and-forget** | Did any team skip more than 2 weekly check-ins this cycle? | Reinstate the heartbeat. Diagnose why it slipped. |
| 11 | **Leadership exempt** | Does the CEO/founder have visible OKRs updated on the same cadence as everyone else? | Founder writes OKRs, references them in all-hands, scores them honestly. |

---

## High severity

| # | Anti-pattern | Self-audit question | Standard fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Too many OKRs** | Does any team have >5 Objectives, or any Objective have >4 KRs? | Cut to 3×3 or smaller. Pick what matters most. |
| 4 | **Sandbagging** | Did any team score >0.85 on every aspirational OKR? | Push next-cycle targets harder. Leadership must publicly tolerate misses. |
| 5 | **Fantasy targets** | Did any team score <0.3 on every aspirational OKR? | Pull targets in. Find a credible (still hard) path to 1.0. |
| 6 | **Vague KRs** | Does any KR lack a baseline, target, metric definition, or deadline? | Sharpen. Run the rubric. |
| 7 | **Necessary-but-not-sufficient KRs** | If we 1.0'd every KR, is it possible the Objective still wasn't achieved? | Add or rewrite KRs until KR success guarantees Objective success. |
| 13 | **Low-Value Objectives** | Would a perfect 1.0 on any Objective go unnoticed by customers, investors, or employees? | Reword to focus on the tangible benefit, or kill the Objective. |
| 16 | **Hidden OKRs** | Are any OKRs not visible to the rest of the org? | Default to public. If the culture won't tolerate that, fix the culture first. |
| 18 | **Initiative drift** | Is the team spending most of its time on work that doesn't ladder to an OKR? | Either change the work or change the OKR. Make the misalignment visible. |
| 19 | **No retro** | Did the cycle end without a structured retro? | Run one. It's the highest-leverage hour of the cycle. |

---

## Medium severity

| # | Anti-pattern | Self-audit question | Standard fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| 9 | **Pure top-down planning** | Were >80% of team OKRs assigned by leadership with no team draft cycle? | Move toward 60/40 (leadership direction / team's own knowledge) next cycle. |
| 10 | **Cascading too deep** | Are there more than 2 levels of OKRs (company → dept → team → squad → individual)? | Cap at company → team for most orgs. |
| 12 | **KPI confusion** | Are any KRs really just KPIs that wouldn't change in response to deliberate effort this cycle? | Either commit to *moving* the KPI as a KR this quarter, or remove it. |
| 14 | **Quarter-1 abandonment** | Did anyone question whether to "drop OKRs because they didn't work" after a single rough cycle? | Plan for 3–4 cycles before judging. Set this expectation explicitly at launch. |
| 15 | **Process bloat** | Has the OKR template, approval workflow, or planning process grown since last cycle? | Cut. Simpler is always better. Teams that launch within one week of planning see ~50% higher completion. |
| 17 | **Mid-cycle silence** | Did the mid-cycle review get skipped or treated as a status update? | Run it. Kill or rewrite OKRs the world has overtaken — that's a sign the practice is alive, not failing. |
| 20 | **Tooling theater** | Is the OKR tool more elaborate than the practice it supports? | Downgrade. The problem is almost never the tooling. |

---

## Program health scoring

| Result | Verdict |
|---|---|
| 0 Critical, ≤2 High | **Healthy.** Keep tuning. |
| 0 Critical, 3–5 High | **Needs work.** Pick the top 2 High items to fix next cycle. |
| Any Critical, OR ≥6 High | **At risk.** Pause ambition. Fix the practice before adding more OKRs. |

A worse rollout that runs honestly beats a polished one that has decoupled from reality.
