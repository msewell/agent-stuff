# OKR Quality Rubric

Use during drafting (owner self-scores) and planning-week review (two peers score independently — disagreements are the most useful diagnostic signal).

## Objective rubric

Score each dimension **0 / 1 / 2**. (0 = fails, 1 = partial, 2 = clear pass.)

**Pass threshold: ≥ 9/12. Any 0 is a hard fail — rewrite before proceeding.**

| # | Dimension | Question | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| O1 | **Inspirational** | Does the Objective make people want to work on it? | Reads like compliance copy | Worthy but flat | Reads like something the team would say unprompted in an all-hands |
| O2 | **Qualitative** | Is the Objective free of numbers? | Loaded with metrics | One stray number | Pure direction, no metrics |
| O3 | **Strategic fit** | Does it ladder to a higher-level Objective or stated strategic priority? | No connection | Implied connection | Explicit parent OKR named |
| O4 | **Time-bounded** | Is it the right scope for one cycle — not a multi-year vision? | Multi-year ambition with no decomposition | Vague timing | Clearly achievable shape within one cycle |
| O5 | **"Who Cares?" test** | If we 1.0'd this, would a customer, investor, or employee notice? | No | Maybe | Yes, with a named audience |
| O6 | **Single-sentence clarity** | Can a new employee understand it in one read? | Needs a paragraph of context | Needs glossary terms | Stands alone |

---

## Key Result rubric

Score each KR independently. **0 / 1 / 2** per dimension.

**Pass threshold: ≥ 12/16. Any 0 on K1, K2, or K8 is a hard fail.**

| # | Dimension | Question | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| K1 | **Outcome, not output** | Could you check this off without a metric moving? | Pure task ("ship X") | Mostly outcome but with a hidden binary | Pure outcome |
| K2 | **Measurable** | Is there a precise metric definition (including cohort, window, data source)? | No | Named metric, ambiguous definition | Named, defined, traceable to a data source |
| K3 | **Baselined** | Is there a current value with a date? | None | Estimate, no date | Concrete number with as-of date |
| K4 | **Targeted** | Is there a specific target value? | "Improve" | Range without commitment | Single number |
| K5 | **Time-bounded** | Is there a deadline? | None | "End of quarter" only | Specific date |
| K6 | **Ambition-calibrated** | Does the team see a credible (but hard) path to 1.0? | Trivially achievable OR clearly impossible | Hard but path is unclear | Hard, with a sketched path |
| K7 | **Non-binary** | Does it admit weekly progress, not just done/not-done? | Binary milestone | Mostly continuous | Continuous with weekly observability |
| K8 | **Sufficient (with peers)** | If this KR and all its siblings hit 1.0, is the Objective achieved? *(Score the set, not the individual.)* | Major gaps | Minor gaps | Yes, sufficient |

---

## OKR-as-a-whole checks

Two final binary checks — fail either and the OKR fails regardless of dimension scores:

1. **Type tagging:** Is the OKR explicitly tagged committed or aspirational? Untagged → fail.
2. **Initiative separation:** Does the OKR document distinguish Key Results (outcomes) from initiatives (the projects believed to drive them)? Initiatives mixed into the KR list → fail.

---

## How to apply the rubric

**During drafting:** Owner self-scores. Anything below threshold gets revised before sharing.

**During planning-week review:** Two peers score independently, then reconcile. Disagreements on a dimension are usually the most useful signal — don't paper them over.

**During mid-cycle review:** Re-run K3 (baselined) and K6 (ambition-calibrated) only. A target that no longer feels right after 6 weeks usually signals new information; rewrite it or tag the OKR as scope-adjusted rather than letting it quietly rot.

**During retro:** Note which dimensions failed most often this cycle. Those are the next-cycle coaching topics.
