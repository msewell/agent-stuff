# Examples and Quick Reference

## The Read-Aloud Test

AI text has a distinctive cadence — metronomic, smooth, featureless. If three
paragraphs have similar sentence lengths and rhythm, the text is too uniform.
Real writing has texture: rhythm shifts, emphasis, the occasional rough edge.

## Before and After Examples

**AI draft:**
> In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, organizations must delve into
> robust strategies that foster innovation and cultivate a culture of meticulous
> excellence. By leveraging cutting-edge technologies, they can embark on a
> transformative journey — one that underscores the pivotal role of seamless
> collaboration in driving sustainable growth.

**After editing:**
> Organizations need better strategies for adopting new technology. That starts
> with making it easier for teams to work together — not with slogans about
> transformation.

What changed: throat-clearing intro deleted, seven flagged words removed, vague
claims replaced with a concrete point, sentence count reduced, and the stance
clarified without overstating the claim.

---

**AI draft:**
> The research highlights the intricate interplay between socioeconomic factors
> and educational outcomes, serving as a testament to the multifaceted nature
> of this complex issue. It's worth noting that these findings could
> potentially reshape how policymakers approach educational reform.

**After editing:**
> The research shows that income and school funding are more tangled than most
> policy assumes. If the findings hold up, they'll force a rethink of how
> reform gets designed.

What changed: "intricate interplay" → specific relationship named, "testament
to the multifaceted nature" deleted, hedging reduced from three layers to one
("if the findings hold up"), passive framing replaced with active.

## Top 10 Tells at a Glance

| # | Tell | Quick Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Flagged vocabulary | Search for "delve," "tapestry," "robust," "pivotal" — any hits? |
| 2 | Em-dash density | More than 3 per 1000 words? |
| 3 | Tricolon abuse | Count three-item lists. More than 2 in a short piece? |
| 4 | Synonym cycling | Does the same concept get a new name every paragraph? |
| 5 | Uniform sentence/paragraph length | Does every block look the same size? |
| 6 | Throat-clearing intro | Does the piece start with "In today's..." or "In the world of..."? |
| 7 | Reflexive summary | Does it end with "In conclusion" when under 1000 words? |
| 8 | Hedging density | Count "arguably," "potentially," "it's worth noting" |
| 9 | Trailing participles | Sentences ending in "-ing" clauses adding vague significance? |
| 10 | Importance puffery | Anything called "pivotal," "watershed," "landmark" without evidence? |
